FLORA OF ASIA

Many of Asia's plant species have great ornamental value. Azaleas, dogwood, primroses, camellias,
peonies, roses, lotus, daisies, cherries, and begonias are frequently planted in gardens. Ornamental
conifers from Asia include pines, spruces, cedars, junipers, umbrella pines, and yews.



NUMBER OF SPECIES

Because Asia is the largest continent, it is not surprising that 100,000 different kinds of plants
grow within its various climate zones, which range from tropical to Arctic. Asian plants, which
include ferns, gymnosperms, and flowering vascular plants, make up 40 percent of the earth's plant
species.

Australia, are thirty-five thousand to forty thousand vascular plant species.
